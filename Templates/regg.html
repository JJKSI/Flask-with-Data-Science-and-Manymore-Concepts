The Mean square error:

MSE is a useful metric for comparing different regression models, as it penalizes large errors more heavily than small ones. A lower MSE indicates that the model is better at making predictions. However, one downside to MSE is that it is measured in the square of the units of the target variable, which may not be intuitive to interpret.

The R square error:

The R2 value ranges from 0 to 1, with higher values indicating that more variance is explained by the model. In the case of linear regression, R-squared tells us how well the linear model fits the data. An R-squared value of 1 indicates that the linear regression model perfectly fits the data, while a value of 0 indicates that the model does not fit the data at all.
